Two Awards, One Documentary — The Story Behind This Is Not A Draft

June 2026  ·  Happy Jack Media

We're proud to announce that This Is Not A Draft took home two awards at the Chandler International Film Festival — and we're still processing what that means.

This film started as a personal project. Jack Blair had a story he wanted to tell — the story of what happens to veterans after service, after the uniform comes off and civilian life begins in earnest. The title comes from a speech by President Obama, and it captures something essential: the lives of these men and women aren't rough drafts. They're the real thing, and they deserve to be treated that way.

From the Arizona Desert to the Film Festival Circuit

A lot of this film was shaped by the landscape. The Arizona desert has a particular quality — it's vast and quiet and a little unforgiving — that mirrors something in the stories we were hearing. Veterans who had seen combat, who had come home and were rebuilding, often described a similar feeling: the scale of what they'd been through versus the smallness of what the civilian world offered in return.

We spent time listening before we ever pointed a camera. That's the approach that shaped everything else.

What Two Awards Mean

Winning at Chandler is meaningful not because of the hardware, but because of what it represents: people sitting in a darkened room, watching this film, and deciding it was worth recognizing. That's the whole point of making something — to have it seen and felt.

"A Post 9/11 Story of Service, Sacrifice & Life After War."

The film is available free on YouTube, Fawesome TV, and Relay. If you haven't seen it, we'd be honored if you did.
